Rahul Dev Burman — universally known as Pancham Da — was the son of legendary composer S.D. Burman. Where his father was rooted in folk and classical tradition, R.D. Burman reached outward: to rock and roll, to bossa nova, to Afro-Cuban rhythms. The result was a catalogue that defined the sound of the 1970s and early 1980s in Hindi cinema.

He worked most memorably with lyricist Gulzar and singer Kishore Kumar — a trio that produced some of the most emotionally precise music Indian cinema has known.
